# Trust Model

Status: Foundational  
Version: 1.0

Momentum becomes more personal through earned trust, not maximal access.

> More trust → voluntary permission → better context → better suggestions → more trust

## Permission by value

Momentum asks for a permission only at a moment when the user can understand the direct benefit.

Examples:

- **Location:** “Share your approximate location to find something meaningful within 15 minutes.”
- **Calendar:** “Let Momentum notice an open hour without reading more detail than it needs.”
- **Health:** “Use recovery signals to avoid suggesting a hard workout when rest may fit better.”

The value explanation appears before the system permission dialog and must be accurate.

## Progressive onboarding

Initial onboarding asks only what cannot be learned responsibly elsewhere and what creates immediate value:

- which experiences or feelings create curiosity;
- what the person wants more of;
- key constraints and accessibility needs;
- their preferred level of initiative.

Permissions are requested progressively, not as a wall of access requests. Declining one never blocks unrelated value.

## Data principles

1. **Minimum necessary:** collect the least precise signal that can provide the benefit.
2. **Purpose bound:** do not reuse context for unrelated goals.
3. **On-device first:** prefer local processing and storage where feasible.
4. **Short-lived by default:** moment context expires unless retention has clear value.
5. **User control:** access, learned preferences, and memories are inspectable, correctable, exportable, and deletable.
6. **No hidden surveillance:** Momentum does not silently ingest private communications or infer relationships from them.
7. **No data-for-engagement model:** personal context is not used to increase compulsive use.

## Explainability without exposure

“Why this?” should show a concise subset of reasons such as time, weather, distance, recovery, or a stated interest. It should not expose sensitive calendar titles, message content, medical details, or information about other people.

## Confidence and correction

Momentum communicates whether a reason is known, inferred, or uncertain. The user can respond with simple corrections such as:

- “Not at work.”
- “I am not with family today.”
- “I still like this, just not now.”
- “Do not use this signal again.”

Corrections take effect immediately.

## Trust failures

When data is stale, a handoff fails, or a claim is wrong, Momentum acknowledges the failure plainly, avoids repeating it, and provides a useful alternative. It never hides behind AI language.

## Non-negotiable boundaries

- No coercive permissions.
- No dark patterns after denial.
- No selling personal context.
- No invented live information.
- No suggestion that impersonates certainty.
- No action on behalf of the user without explicit authorization appropriate to its impact.
