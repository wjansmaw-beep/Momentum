# International Content Architecture

Status: Directional, implemented prototype contract  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

Momentum must remain useful anywhere without pretending that every place has the same live coverage or local knowledge. The content system therefore separates:

- the global decision engine;
- the device language and content language;
- geographic coverage;
- hemisphere and season;
- global evergreen experiences;
- reviewed regional and local content packs;
- independent live-source coverage.

Location affects which content is eligible. It does not change the Human Moment, ranking, Capsule, Trust, or four-surface architecture.

## Resolution order

For the active approximate coordinates Momentum resolves content in this order:

> local pack -> regional pack -> global evergreen pack

The results are additive. A matching local pack enriches the global basis; it never replaces the fallback. If no trusted local or regional pack exists, Momentum shows complete global experiences and does not invent local character.

Local and regional packs are eligible only when the coordinates represent a user-confirmed approximate environment. A development or validation region may demonstrate source behavior, but it may not influence personal selection as if it were the user's location.

## Global evergreen standard

An evergreen experience must:

- work without a live-world claim;
- require no specific city, country, weather pattern, venue, or culture;
- include a complete Capsule or honest handoff;
- remain useful with minimal permissions;
- avoid assuming season from a northern-hemisphere calendar;
- state any equipment or company requirement before selection.

The first Dutch-language global pack covers food, movement, restoration, connection, learning, culture, and broadly available outdoor experiences. Product language is still a Founder decision; global geography does not imply that localization is complete.

## Local pack standard

A local pack is editorial content with bounded geographic coverage. Each pack declares:

- stable identifier and title;
- local, regional, or global scope;
- content language;
- bounded coverage;
- optional valid seasons;
- experience identifiers;
- editorial or curator provenance.

Local packs may describe local meaning, but live claims such as current bloom, sightings, opening status, weather, or events must still come through Live World with provenance and freshness.

## Safe fallback

Missing local content is not an error. The app falls back automatically to global evergreen experiences while live sources continue to report their own independent coverage. No Dutch coastal experience may appear outside its approved bounds merely because it ranks highly.

## Prototype validation regions

- Dokkum validates local plus global composition.
- New York validates global fallback in a dense North American city.
- Tokyo validates global fallback in a different language and cultural context.

These are scenario checks, not market launches or claims of local coverage.

## Next expansion

1. Validate whether the first global experiences are culturally and practically portable with users.
2. Choose the initial product languages and add complete language packs rather than partial translation.
3. Add one reviewed regional pack with a named local curator or verifiable editorial source.
4. Keep local packs small until their suggestions demonstrably lead to worthwhile experiences.
