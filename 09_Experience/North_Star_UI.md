# North Star UI

Status: Directional, not a pixel specification  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

The North Star UI describes the ideal first 30 seconds of Momentum. It is a test for future visual design, not a fixed screen layout.

## The opening moment

Momentum opens directly inside the user's present context. It does not lead with a logo sequence, dashboard, greeting, category grid, or chatbot prompt.

Within three seconds the user should perceive:

- one experience that may be worth choosing now;
- the feeling or moment it promises;
- one truthful detail that makes it distinctive;
- the practical size of the commitment;
- one clear path to begin;
- a quiet way to choose differently.

## Emotional sequence

### 0–3 seconds: desire

The Living Canvas and Experience Promise let the user imagine being there, doing it, or feeling its outcome. The screen is calm because only one idea asks for attention.

### 3–8 seconds: orientation

Compact facts answer “Can this fit?” Time, distance, cost, weather, equipment, or effort appear only when material.

### 8–15 seconds: trust and agency

“Why this fits” is available without dominating. The user can accept, decline, or say where they have room now. Momentum's confidence feels honest rather than authoritative.

### 15–30 seconds: commitment

Accepting the promise transforms it into an Experience Capsule. Preparation appears in the same visual world, with one next action. The interface does not reward further browsing.

### After commitment: release

Momentum hands off or reduces itself to the minimum guide. The visual experience dissolves into Presence.

## Why it feels personal

Personal does not mean displaying everything Momentum knows. It means the suggestion, timing, promise, and effort fit the person without exposing private context. A short explanation may reference time, distance, weather, stated preference, or permitted recovery context.

## Why it feels premium

Premium quality comes from restraint and precision:

- a strong editorial choice instead of abundance;
- accurate, well-sourced media and language;
- seamless state transitions;
- excellent typography, spacing, accessibility, and performance;
- no advertising clutter, manipulation, or generic AI voice;
- graceful failure when context is missing.

## Why it feels calm

The interface has one visual center, limited copy, deliberate motion, and no feed. Alternatives exist to preserve autonomy, not to create comparison anxiety.

## Why it is not social media

There is no endless content, public performance, popularity ranking, streak pressure, autoplay loop, or reward for staying. The action leaves the product; the memory belongs to the user.

## States the North Star must support

- a strong, high-confidence promise;
- a useful general promise with little permissioned context;
- no responsible suggestion, followed by one small question or silence;
- active intent such as “I have one hour”;
- decline and change direction without friction;
- reduced motion, screen reader, low bandwidth, and no imagery;
- a failed or changed live-world opportunity.

## Evaluation questions

- Does the first glance create truthful desire rather than explain an algorithm?
- Can the commitment be understood before acceptance?
- Can the user choose otherwise without fighting the interface?
- Does accepting lead to action rather than another content page?
- Does the product become quieter within 30 seconds?
- Would the user remember the experience more than this screen?
