# Living Canvas

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

The Living Canvas is the emotional field behind the primary Experience Promise. It helps the user picture the future experience before reading its explanation. It is neither a mandatory hero image nor decoration.

The canvas should create desire within a glance, then yield attention to the action.

## Principles

- **Promise before information:** communicate atmosphere or felt outcome before data.
- **Truth before spectacle:** use visuals that honestly represent the likely experience.
- **Life, not content:** never become a video feed or a reason to linger.
- **Quiet motion:** movement suggests a living world without demanding attention.
- **Adaptive feeling:** visual energy follows the intended state, not a fixed activity category.
- **Accessible by default:** meaning remains available without motion, color, or imagery.

## Choosing the medium

### Photography

Use when a real place, dish, person-approved memory, or material detail is central to the promise. Prefer current, credited, representative imagery over generic stock photography.

### Illustration

Use when privacy, abstraction, emotional safety, unavailable photography, or a guided inner experience makes literal imagery inappropriate.

### Subtle video or cinemagraph

Use only when motion materially conveys the moment—moving water, changing light, breath, weather. It must be brief or ambient, muted, battery-aware, and replaceable with a still image.

### Map

Use as a spatial preview when orientation creates confidence. It should not lead the primary promise unless the route itself is the emotional experience.

### No representational image

Use light, typography, texture, or color when calm, breathing, reflection, sensitive connection, or uncertainty would be weakened by an image.

### Live Activity

A Live Activity is not a hero medium. It is a minimal continuation after the experience starts: remaining time, the next essential cue, or a meaningful time window.

## Motion grammar

Motion should express one of three meanings:

- **alive:** subtle environmental movement;
- **commitment:** the promise expands into its capsule;
- **release:** interface elements dissolve as Presence begins.

Avoid loops, parallax, celebration, or novelty animation that rewards staying. Respect reduced motion and provide equivalent static hierarchy.

## Content integrity

AI may select or crop an approved visual; it may not silently fabricate documentary evidence. Generated imagery must not be presented as a current real-world view. Time-sensitive visual claims require source and freshness metadata.

## Failure states

If media is unavailable, stale, slow, inaccessible, or untrustworthy, the experience falls back gracefully to typography and a calm color field. The promise must remain understandable and actionable without media.

## Review test

The canvas succeeds when it makes the experience felt quickly, supports truthful choice, and becomes forgettable once the user decides to act.
