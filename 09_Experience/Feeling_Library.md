# Feeling Library

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

Momentum recommends experiences because of the human state they may support, not because activities belong to a category. The Feeling Library gives product, design, and intelligence teams a shared language for those intended outcomes.

Feelings are invitations and hypotheses—not guaranteed results, diagnoses, or scores the user must achieve.

## From feeling to experience

The Understanding Engine combines a desired or inferred state with context and selects a feasible experience. Wonder expresses the promise honestly. The same activity may serve different outcomes:

- a walk can support calm after work, wonder at sunset, connection with family, or energy in the morning;
- cooking can support nourishment, creativity, comfort, or togetherness;
- training can support vitality, pride, challenge, or flow.

The promise and presentation must reflect the intended outcome rather than applying one fixed emotional label to an activity.

## Core feeling families

### Calm

**Human need:** less mental noise or physical tension.  
**Experience qualities:** simple, predictable, gentle, low preparation.  
**Language:** spacious, reassuring, non-clinical.  
**Visual rhythm:** low contrast, slow transitions, room to breathe.  
**Possible experiences:** breathing, an unhurried walk, tea, quiet music, a short reset.

### Wonder

**Human need:** feel awake to the world.  
**Experience qualities:** timely, distinctive, sensory, discoverable.  
**Language:** specific and evocative without exaggeration.  
**Visual rhythm:** depth, light, a revealing detail.  
**Possible experiences:** unusual light, wildlife, art, stars, seasonal nature, an overlooked place.

### Energy

**Human need:** feel activated and ready.  
**Experience qualities:** immediate, achievable, rhythmic.  
**Language:** concise and encouraging, never pressuring.  
**Visual rhythm:** clearer contrast and slightly faster motion.  
**Possible experiences:** a brief workout, morning shake, music, daylight, brisk movement.

### Pride

**Human need:** experience earned capability.  
**Experience qualities:** clear challenge, achievable finish, visible progress.  
**Language:** confident but not competitive.  
**Possible experiences:** strength work, learning a skill, finishing a route, making something.

### Connection

**Human need:** give or receive meaningful attention.  
**Experience qualities:** shared, personal, interruption-light.  
**Language:** warm and optional; never guilt-based.  
**Possible experiences:** a walk together, cooking, calling someone, a small shared ritual.

### Curiosity

**Human need:** explore an idea, place, or perspective.  
**Experience qualities:** a clear question, low entry threshold, room for discovery.  
**Possible experiences:** museum detail, local story, book passage, workshop, unfamiliar route.

### Adventure

**Human need:** novelty, freedom, and manageable uncertainty.  
**Experience qualities:** safe enough to begin, open enough to feel new.  
**Language:** inviting rather than reckless.  
**Possible experiences:** spontaneous day trip, new trail, local event, unfamiliar food.

### Restoration

**Human need:** recover capacity rather than perform.  
**Experience qualities:** low demand, appropriate duration, permission to stop.  
**Language:** supportive and evidence-calibrated.  
**Possible experiences:** rest, sleep preparation, gentle mobility, nature, doing nothing.

### Gratitude

**Human need:** notice value already present.  
**Experience qualities:** reflective, concrete, brief.  
**Possible experiences:** save one photo, revisit a memory, name one good moment, thank someone.

### Flow

**Human need:** absorbed attention in a fitting challenge.  
**Experience qualities:** clear next step, minimal interruption, balanced difficulty.  
**Possible experiences:** cooking, training, making, photography, focused learning.

## Emotional safeguards

Momentum does not deliberately create:

- fear of missing out or manufactured urgency;
- guilt about inactivity, food, family, or self-care;
- shame, comparison, or productivity pressure;
- dependency on approval from the app;
- overstimulation disguised as energy;
- certainty about a feeling the system cannot guarantee.

## Selection guidance

Each Experience Promise has one primary feeling and at most two supporting feelings. If the system cannot state why an experience plausibly supports the outcome, the feeling label is decorative and should be removed.

Explicit intent outweighs an inferred desired state. A user may reject or correct any emotional interpretation without changing their identity profile permanently.

## Learning

After an experience, Momentum may ask one proportional question such as “How do you feel now?” The answer may improve future matching, but it must not turn wellbeing into a performance dashboard. The absence of feedback means unknown, not failure.
