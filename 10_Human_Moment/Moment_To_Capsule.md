# Moment to Capsule

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This document describes the final translation from a recognized Human Moment to a temporary Experience Capsule. A capsule is generated only after the moment is understood and an experience is selected.

> Human Moment → Understanding → candidates → selection → Experience Promise → user choice → Experience Capsule → Presence → Memory

The capsule is not a generic activity page. It is the smallest complete guide for this person, in this moment, to carry out the chosen experience.

## Translation rules

1. Preserve explicit intent and constraints.
2. Carry only verified, relevant context into the promise.
3. Show the real commitment before acceptance.
4. Ask for permission when its value becomes concrete.
5. After acceptance, reveal preparation rather than more persuasion.
6. Use trusted platform capabilities where they simplify execution.
7. Reduce guidance as soon as the person can be present.
8. Never claim completion without adequate evidence or confirmation.

## Example 1 — Unexpected hour at work: workout

**Human Moment:** a cancelled meeting creates 55 minutes; the user can train at work.  
**Intent:** challenge, remain on-site.  
**Candidates:** kettlebell strength, bodyweight interval, brisk loop.  
**Selection:** a 32-minute kettlebell session with low setup and return buffer.  
**Promise:** "One kettlebell and a clear finish. You have enough time for a demanding session without rushing back."  
**Capsule:** equipment check → warm-up → one exercise or interval at a time → finish → optional felt outcome.  
**Presence:** screen remains minimal; haptics or timer carry the sequence.

## Example 2 — Open evening: recipe

**Human Moment:** enough time before dinner, at home, low willingness to shop.  
**Intent:** something warm and simple.  
**Selection:** a recipe using available ingredients, subject to reliable inventory input.  
**Promise:** "In 25 minutes you could be eating something warm and familiar, without another trip out."  
**Capsule:** ingredient confirmation → substitutions → one step at a time → timers → serve → optional memory.

## Example 3 — Brief evening restoration

**Human Moment:** ten quiet minutes before sleep.  
**Intent:** calm, no achievement.  
**Candidates:** brief breathing, gentle stretch, or a quiet pause.  
**Selection:** a five-minute guide only if guidance is wanted.  
**Promise:** "Nothing needs to be achieved. Five quiet minutes could help the evening slow down."  
**Capsule:** settle → begin rhythm → dim screen → subtle cues → quiet completion.

## Example 4 — Shared nearby nature

**Human Moment:** an open hour together, favorable conditions, a verified nearby viewpoint.  
**Intent:** something easy to discover together.  
**Promise:** "You have an open hour together. The water should be quiet near sunset, twelve minutes away."  
**Capsule:** what to take → route handoff → arrival cue → silence → optional shared photo.  
**Presence:** Apple Maps may navigate; leaving Momentum does not leave the experience.

## Example 5 — Unfamiliar city: culture

**Human Moment:** 75 minutes while travelling, on foot, with a verified exhibition nearby.  
**Intent:** curiosity without a full itinerary.  
**Capsule:** essential details → ticket or entry handoff → route → one arrival prompt → silence → optional memory.

## Example 6 — No suitable experience

When context is conflicting or stale and no candidate is trustworthy or worthwhile, Momentum asks one small question, offers a permission-light pause, or remains silent. No capsule is created.

Abstention protects the product promise. Not every moment needs to be filled.

## Continuity across handoffs

The experience identity, promise, and essential state should remain coherent when a system capability opens. Momentum resumes only when the platform and permissions support that reliably. Otherwise it waits for the user rather than simulating continuity.

## Completion test

The chosen experience follows from the moment, its promise is honest, commitment is visible, acceptance starts preparation, handoffs feel purposeful, guidance becomes quieter, and Memory remains optional.
