# Moment Taxonomy

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This taxonomy gives Momentum a shared language for recognizing human situations without reducing people to fixed categories. Moment types are temporary hypotheses, may overlap, and must expire as context changes.

## Three distinct layers

### Context signals

Observations or user-provided facts such as 45 minutes available, being at work, dry weather, company, preparation tolerance, or recent activity. These are ingredients, not moments.

### Human moments

Situations with practical or emotional meaning, such as an unexpected opening, a transition after work, a shared family window, a waiting interval, an evening wind-down, or a brief living-world opportunity.

### Intent and desired state

What the user explicitly adds, such as calm, challenge, connection, surprise, food, movement, or staying nearby. An inferred need must remain a cautious hypothesis and never masquerade as explicit intent.

## Primary moment families

### 1. Openings

- unexpected free 15–90 minutes;
- a cancelled commitment or earlier finish;
- an open morning, afternoon, evening, or weekend window.

### 2. Transitions

- starting the day or taking lunch;
- leaving work or arriving home;
- preparing for sleep;
- returning from travel.

### 3. Shared moments

- time with partner, family, child, or friend;
- an unexpected opportunity to reconnect;
- hosting or eating together;
- different capacities within one group.

Company is used only when explicitly known or responsibly provided.

### 4. Capacity-shaped moments

- ready for challenge;
- enough capacity for something light;
- low preparation tolerance;
- depleted or restored after effort.

Momentum describes capacity cautiously and does not make medical interpretations.

### 5. Place-shaped moments

- at work with limited equipment;
- close to home or already outdoors;
- in an unfamiliar city;
- waiting at a station or appointment;
- near a personally meaningful place.

### 6. Living-world opportunities

- brief favorable weather;
- sunset, tide, blossom, migration, or seasonal change;
- a verified local event;
- temporary quiet or accessibility;
- a fresh food or cultural opportunity.

Live claims require provenance, freshness, and responsible handling of sensitive places.

### 7. Restoration and pause

- a brief reset between obligations;
- quiet after overstimulation;
- an evening wind-down;
- permission to do nothing;
- gentle recovery after effort.

### 8. Self-directed moments

- "I have half an hour";
- "surprise me nearby";
- "we want to do something together";
- "I want to cook with what I have";
- "give me a challenge."

These statements should be preserved as explicit input, not converted into inferred identity.

## Composition

A moment can combine families:

> unexpected opening + at work + ready for challenge + must stay nearby

The composition is more useful than a single label. Labels help organize reasoning; they must not determine the result by themselves.

## Anti-taxonomy

Momentum must not create fixed person types such as "lazy," "unhealthy," "adventurous person," or "bad sleeper." It also avoids moment labels designed for engagement, guilt, vulnerability exploitation, or advertising.

The taxonomy describes the present opportunity. It does not define the person.
