# Experience Ranking

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

Ranking compares already feasible experiences for one Human Moment. It does not calculate human worth, prescribe the optimal life, or maximize engagement.

## Ranking priorities

Hard constraints are applied before ranking. Among viable candidates, consider:

1. **Explicit intent fit:** present-tense user input is the strongest directional signal.
2. **Feasibility:** the full experience fits time, place, preparation, cost, equipment, accessibility, and conditions.
3. **Likely meaningful outcome:** the experience plausibly supports the desired feeling or valued pattern.
4. **Moment fit:** timing, transition, company, and setting make the experience appropriate now.
5. **Friction:** beginning and completing can be made genuinely simple.
6. **Trust quality:** reasons are sourced, fresh, explainable, and proportionate to permissions used.
7. **Novelty and repetition:** enough variation without sacrificing relevance or familiar value.
8. **Presence potential:** technology can move into the background quickly.
9. **Memory potential:** there is a plausible meaningful outcome without forced documentation.

## Factors that must not increase rank

- expected screen time or app return;
- commercial value, sponsorship, or affiliate revenue;
- emotional vulnerability;
- guilt, streak preservation, or fear of missing out;
- unnecessary use of more personal data;
- the system's desire to prove its intelligence.

Commercial influence may never be hidden inside personal ranking.

## Confidence and margin

Track both confidence that the selected experience is feasible and suitable, and the difference between leading candidates. A narrow margin means the choice is fragile. Momentum may use more modest language, offer a small redirect, or ask one discriminating question.

## Diversity over time

Variety is evaluated across a period, not forced into every moment. Familiar experiences may rightly win. Novelty rises only when the user values discovery, feasibility stays strong, repetition is reducing value, and the new experience has adequate trust and capsule quality.

## User control

Users can correct ranking with ordinary statements such as "Not while I am at work," "I want outdoors," or "I still like this, just not today." Corrections affect the current selection immediately and durable learning only when intended.

## Evaluation

Ranking quality should eventually ask whether the user understood and freely chose the suggestion, it was feasible as promised, beginning was simple, technology became quieter, the experience felt worthwhile, and the explanation felt accurate rather than invasive.

Acceptance alone is insufficient; rejection alone does not prove poor ranking.
