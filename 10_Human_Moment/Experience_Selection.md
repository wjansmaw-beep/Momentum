# Experience Selection

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

Experience Selection converts a Human Moment and any explicit intent into a small set of feasible candidates, then chooses at most one primary experience to present.

> Of the experiences that could genuinely work now, which one best serves this moment?

## Pipeline

> Human Moment + Intent → candidate generation → hard filtering → comparative ranking → one selection or abstention → Experience Promise

### 1. Define the opportunity

Summarize the moment without naming an activity: time window, setting, capacity, company, constraints, desired state, and relevant world conditions.

### 2. Generate across experience forms

Candidates may include movement, food, restoration, nature, culture, learning, connection, or consciously doing nothing. Candidate generation should not be trapped by legacy PRIMX modules or by the most obvious activity.

### 3. Apply hard filters

Remove candidates that are impossible within total time, unavailable or stale, incompatible with known constraints, dependent on unnecessary permissions, explicitly ruled out for this moment, or missing a viable capsule or trusted handoff.

### 4. Compare viable candidates

Use the factors in `Experience_Ranking.md`. Ranking is comparative and explainable; it is not a universal score for a person's life.

### 5. Select or abstain

Present one primary Experience Promise when it is sufficiently feasible, relevant, and distinct. Otherwise ask one useful question, offer a low-context fallback, or show no recommendation.

## Candidate contract

Each candidate should conceptually contain:

- experience form and intended felt outcome;
- fit with moment and explicit intent;
- total commitment: preparation, travel, activity, and return buffer;
- constraints, cost, equipment, accessibility, and safety needs;
- live facts with provenance and freshness;
- novelty and repetition history;
- capsule readiness and handoff plan;
- reasons for, reasons against, and uncertainty;
- privacy cost and required permission;
- fallback when conditions change.

## Active and passive examples

### Passive

Moment: an open hour near home before sunset, with known interest in nature. Candidates include a quiet viewpoint, short neighborhood walk, mobility session, or early meal preparation. Nature does not automatically win.

### Active

Moment: an unexpected 55-minute work break. Intent: "I want a challenge and can train here." Candidates include a kettlebell session, bodyweight interval, or brisk outdoor loop. Explicit intent reshapes the same selection process.

## Alternatives

The primary suggestion remains visually dominant. A user may decline, refine the moment, or request another direction. Alternatives preserve autonomy without becoming an infinite feed.

"Not now" is about this moment unless the user explicitly changes a durable preference.
