# Intent

Status: Foundational  
Version: 1.0

Intent represents what the user wants, needs, or has room for now. It complements contextual understanding because no device can fully know a person's present inner state.

## Passive intent

Momentum infers a possible need from permitted context and presents one suggestion. Examples: recovery after a demanding day, a meal near dinner time, or a nearby experience during an open afternoon.

Passive intent is always a hypothesis, never a command.

## Active intent

The user may correct or enrich the moment with a lightweight statement such as:

- “I unexpectedly have one hour.”
- “I need some calm.”
- “We want to do something together.”
- “Surprise me nearby.”

The preferred interaction asks:

> Where do you have room for now?

The smallest useful inputs are time, desired feeling, company, and a necessary constraint. Momentum should not force users through a catalogue before it can help.

## Autonomy controls

Every suggestion supports three outcomes:

- **accept:** turn it into an Experience Capsule;
- **decline or not now:** preserve autonomy and learn cautiously;
- **change direction:** provide active intent or request another suitable option.

A decline is not a negative score against the person's identity. It can reflect timing, mood, incomplete context, repetition, or simple choice.

## Alternatives and exploration

Momentum should provide space to choose something else without letting alternatives compete visually with the primary promise.

Exploration is context-aware inspiration, not an infinite feed. It should begin from a desired state or available moment and return a small, meaningful set.

## Intent safeguards

- Never infer sensitive intent from weak signals.
- Never use guilt or pressure after a rejection.
- Never keep resurfacing an overridden recommendation.
- Let the user inspect, correct, or remove learned preferences.
- Treat explicit present-tense intent as more authoritative than a conflicting inference, subject to safety constraints.
