# ADR-025 — Direction and learning live inside experiences

Status: Accepted direction, interaction unresolved  
Date: 2026-07-13

## Decision

Momentum may use explicitly chosen near intentions, growth directions, and meaning anchors to improve selection and continuity. It may also provide source-grounded learning inside an Experience Capsule when that knowledge deepens what the person is already doing.

Purpose, Improvement, and Learn do not return as legacy modules or primary navigation. Their value is integrated across Now, Today, Discover, Capsules, Memory, and a future user-controlled direction editor.

## Why

The current prototype can recommend activities but underrepresents two parts of the product vision:

1. experiences can contribute to short-term intentions and long-term meaning;
2. Momentum can itself explain and guide, rather than outsourcing learning to “read a book.”

The integrated model preserves the four approved surfaces and the Experience Loop while making experiences richer and more personal.

## Boundaries

- Meaning anchors are user-authored or explicitly confirmed.
- Goals guide ranking but never create guilt, streak pressure, or automatic failure.
- Information is sparse, timely, optional, and source-grounded.
- Presence and safety outrank education.
- Generated synthesis is distinguishable from verified facts and live observations.
- No final UI, data retention period, or AI generation architecture is approved by this decision.

## Consequences

Experience content needs a future Meaning Thread and information-provenance contract. The personal model needs separate, reversible fields for near intentions, growth directions, and meaning anchors before these signals may influence production ranking.
