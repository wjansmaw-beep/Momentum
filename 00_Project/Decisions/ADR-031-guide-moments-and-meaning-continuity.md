# ADR-031 — Guide moments and meaning continuity

Status: Accepted and first interaction prototyped  
Date: 2026-07-15

## Decision

Representative Capsules may contain up to three optional, stage-specific guide moments. They explain something that improves the experience already underway: what to notice, why a cooking choice works, what a movement is for, how to stay oriented, or how to look and listen with more attention.

Preparation previews the selected guide depth and names the kind of guidance available. Presence still shows only the current step. The consultable guide reveals current source evidence in guide and deep modes, links to the original source, and includes the observation time. Deep mode may collect the remaining guide moments, but does not create a separate reading destination.

Memory may carry a short, optional trace of at most two guide-moment titles. This closes the Meaning Thread without requiring a journal entry or assuming that the supplied information was meaningful.

## Why

The layered guide in ADR-030 had structural depth, but representative experiences often contained only one insight. That made deep mode technically different but experientially thin. Multiple stage-specific moments make recipes, workouts, routes, family play, recovery, learning, and culture feel like a living guide while keeping information subordinate to action.

## Boundaries

- No guide moment is inserted merely to increase content volume.
- The current activity and safety remain primary.
- Editorial language stays conservative and cannot become diagnosis, guaranteed outcomes, invented local history, or fabricated live context.
- A live claim links to its recorded source and shows its observation time.
- Muted topics remain absent from the consultable guide.
- Memory language says what may have stayed with the person; it does not claim learning or growth.
