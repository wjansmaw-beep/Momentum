# ADR-050 — Founder Generator Coverage Matrix

Status: Accepted and implemented  
Version: 1.0  
Date: 2026-07-18

## Problem

The Founder evaluation bench can create any individual test Capsule, but raw per-kind totals do not reveal which materially different contexts remain untested. Repeatedly choosing familiar contexts can create a false sense of generator breadth.

## Decision

Momentum Lab maintains a small, explicit coverage plan of fourteen core scenarios: two materially different contexts for each of the seven approved experience kinds. The scenarios vary time, day part, company, and—where relevant—equipment.

The Lab recommends one next missing scenario. One action opens that scenario as exactly one complete ordinary Capsule. Founder evaluation still happens through Promise, Prepare, Presence, and Memory rather than through a draft grid or text-only review.

The Founder may also compose a free test. A free test remains visible in general generator evidence, while only an exact core-scenario match advances the coverage matrix.

## Evidence boundary

Each local trial records only:

- the explicit test scenario;
- whether a valid Capsule was shown or rejected;
- the generated Experience identifier;
- optional post-experience generator-quality signals.

Trials remain local, are capped, and do not affect the personal profile, ranking preference, memories, ordinary context, or production analytics.

## Interpretation

Completing the matrix proves coverage of the chosen prototype contexts, not general model quality or real-user value. A scenario with few positive signals becomes eligible for retesting after the initial matrix is complete. Absence of a positive signal is not converted into a negative personal preference.

## Product boundary

The coverage planner belongs only in Momentum Lab. Consumer surfaces retain one best suggestion, a finite direct alternative, and deliberate Discover. The matrix is a product-development instrument, not a user checklist or engagement mechanism.
