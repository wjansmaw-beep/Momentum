# ADR-014: Momentum has four human surfaces

Status: Accepted  
Date: 2026-07-12

## Context

The North Star correctly protects `Now` from becoming a feed. Applying that restraint to the entire product, however, leaves too little room for anticipating the day, expressing an active intent, or revisiting lived experience.

The Founder reviewed the visual concepts and approved a broader product structure that preserves one primary suggestion while giving deliberate choice a clear home.

## Decision

Momentum has four primary surfaces:

1. **Now** presents at most one primary Experience Promise for the current Human Moment.
2. **Today** reveals a small number of time-bound opportunities across the day. It is a quiet day rhythm, not a schedule optimizer or feed.
3. **Discover** is consciously opened. It supports `Surprise me`, guided expression, and the user's own words. Results are finite and contextual.
4. **Life Book** preserves meaningful memories and repeatable experiences without performance mechanics.

Profile, permissions, learned preferences, and prototype context remain supporting controls rather than a primary life destination.

All surfaces converge on the same Experience Promise and Experience Capsule contract.

## Choice policy

- `Now`: one suggestion or respectful silence.
- `Today`: one opportunity per meaningful time window, normally no more than four.
- `Discover`: one recommendation and at most one genuinely different alternative.
- `Life Book`: user-owned memories, not recommendations.

## Consequences

- ADR-012 still prohibits a feed on `Now`, but no longer defers a bounded Discover destination.
- The active-intent interaction must accept free expression and cannot be limited to a fixed feeling list.
- Today orchestration and intent expression become explicit design contracts.
- Existing Capsule and Presence rules remain unchanged.
