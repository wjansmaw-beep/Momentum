# Prototype Content Set — Unexpected Free Hour

Status: Directional test content  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

This content set gives the first interaction prototype enough variety to prove that the same Human Moment can produce different experience forms. It is not a production catalogue and contains no claim of live personalization.

## Selection principle

The prototype begins with:

> I have one hour.

One desired outcome changes the proposal. Each outcome must lead to a complete capsule that fits within the hour including preparation and transition buffer.

## Scenario A — Come to rest

### Promise

> **Let the pace fall away before the hour is gone.**  
> Ten minutes of gentle movement followed by five quiet breaths gives you a complete pause without turning rest into another task.

Commitment: 18 minutes, no equipment, no travel.  
Primary action: **Take this pause**

### Capsule

Settle → gentle movement → breathing rhythm → screen dims → quiet completion.

### Presence

After the rhythm begins, only subtle timing remains. The user may stop guidance and continue in silence.

## Scenario B — Feel energized

### Promise

> **Move enough to change the rest of the afternoon.**  
> A brisk 22-minute session uses only the space around you and leaves more than half the hour unclaimed.

Commitment: 26 minutes including setup, no assumed equipment or travel.  
Primary action: **Begin moving**

### Capsule

Readiness confirmation → short warm-up → simple bodyweight sequence → clear finish → optional felt outcome.

### Safety boundary

The prototype does not claim the session is medically appropriate or use invented recovery data. A production version requires appropriate readiness, accessibility, and health safeguards.

## Scenario C — Be surprised

### Permission-light promise

> **Notice something nearby you usually pass.**  
> Take a short observation walk and look for one detail worth remembering: light, architecture, nature, or sound.

Commitment: 30 minutes, begins from the current place, no live location claim.  
Primary action: **Give me the prompt**

### Capsule

Choose an accessible direction → one observation prompt → phone away → return when ready → optionally keep one photo or sentence.

### Why this version

Without location permission or verified Living World data, Momentum must not invent a nearby Hidden Gem. Wonder comes from a truthful way of looking, not a fabricated live fact.

## Scenario D — Challenge myself

### Promise

> **Give the hour one clear finish.**  
> A focused 24-minute bodyweight challenge has a defined endpoint and enough buffer to recover before whatever comes next.

Commitment: 30 minutes including warm-up and transition.  
Primary action: **Set up the challenge**

### Capsule

Readiness and movement options → warm-up → timed rounds → cooldown → complete.

### Correction

The user can reduce intensity without rejecting the desired feeling of challenge.

## Scenario E — Feel connected

### Promise

> **Give someone ten undistracted minutes.**  
> Choose the person yourself; Momentum will offer one question worth asking and then disappear.

Commitment: 10–20 minutes, no contact or communication access required.  
Primary action: **Give me the question**

### Capsule

User chooses their own contact method → one conversation prompt → Momentum disappears → no monitoring of the conversation.

### Privacy boundary

Momentum does not infer whom to call, whether another person is available, or what has been discussed.

## Scenario F — Choose for me

With no optional context, Momentum should not pretend to know which of the above is best. The prototype may use a transparent, reversible editorial rule:

- prefer the lowest-friction complete experience;
- avoid repeating the last accepted prototype scenario;
- state the limited basis plainly.

Example:

> **Here is one simple way to make the hour feel different.**  
> I do not know enough to personalize this yet, so I chose something that starts where you are and needs no equipment.

Primary action: **Try this**  
Correction: **Choose a feeling instead**

## Abstention state

When the user adds a constraint that invalidates the available prototype capsules, Momentum responds:

> **I do not see a good fit without guessing.**  
> You can leave the hour open, or tell me whether you want calm, energy, or surprise.

There is no fake recommendation and no permission pressure.

## Prototype limitations

- All scenario inputs are explicit or labelled mock context.
- No real Apple, health, calendar, weather, location, or Living World integration is implied.
- Copy is test content, not a validated wellbeing claim.
- These scenarios test interaction breadth, not final content quality.
- Production selection and safety behavior require later technical and domain validation.
