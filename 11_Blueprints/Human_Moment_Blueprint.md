# Human Moment Blueprint

Status: Directional  
Version: 1.0

## Purpose

A Human Moment Blueprint tests whether Momentum can turn one recognizable life situation into a relevant, trustworthy, and executable experience without choosing the activity in advance.

It is a product-design instrument, not a screen specification or technical schema.

## Blueprint sequence

### 1. Human situation

Describe the moment in ordinary language before naming data or activities.

Questions:

- What changed or became possible?
- Why might the person open Momentum now?
- When does this opportunity expire?
- What must Momentum not assume?

### 2. Known context

Separate all inputs into:

- **explicit:** stated or corrected by the user;
- **observed:** current permitted facts with source and freshness;
- **learned:** editable preferences or prior outcomes;
- **inferred:** cautious hypotheses;
- **unknown:** missing information that could change the result.

### 3. Smallest clarification

Ask nothing when the current context is enough for a responsible proposal. Ask one question only when its answer would materially change feasibility, desired outcome, or safety.

The question should concern the present moment, not force profile administration.

### 4. Opportunity statement

Summarize what the moment creates without selecting an activity.

Example:

> There is room for one low-preparation experience nearby that can be completed without making the return to work feel rushed.

### 5. Candidate field

Generate meaningfully different ways to serve the moment. Include at least one quiet or low-effort candidate when appropriate, and allow doing nothing to remain valid.

For each candidate record:

- intended felt outcome;
- total time and return buffer;
- practical requirements;
- strongest reason for;
- strongest reason against;
- uncertainty and source quality;
- capsule and handoff readiness.

### 6. Hard filters

Remove candidates that cannot truthfully fit the complete commitment or known constraints. Filtering happens before preference ranking.

### 7. Comparative selection

Compare remaining candidates using `10_Human_Moment/Experience_Ranking.md`. The result is:

- one primary candidate;
- one short explanation of why it leads;
- confidence and ranking margin;
- or abstention when no candidate deserves the promise.

### 8. Experience Promise

Translate the selected candidate into:

> Feeling → specific detail → why now → action

The promise creates truthful desire while showing enough commitment to make a free choice.

### 9. User agency

Always support:

- accept;
- not now;
- change one part of the moment;
- request another direction.

The alternative path must not become an infinite feed.

### 10. Experience Capsule

After acceptance, persuasion stops. The promise becomes the smallest complete preparation and guidance flow. Technology becomes quieter as action begins.

### 11. Presence and Memory

State when Momentum disappears, how an external capability may help, what failure looks like, and whether one lightweight reflection is appropriate.

## Validation

A blueprint is ready for prototyping when:

- the activity is visibly an outcome rather than the premise;
- explicit intent and inference are distinguishable;
- at least several plausible candidates were genuinely compared;
- time includes preparation, travel, activity, and return buffer;
- the selected promise is attractive without exaggeration;
- the user can redirect without rebuilding a profile;
- the capsule leads out of browsing and into action;
- missing data, declined permissions, and abstention are covered.
