# Today Orchestration

Status: Directional interaction blueprint  
Version: 1.0

## Human moment

A person wants help noticing worthwhile openings across the day without surrendering the day to an optimizer.

## Contract

Today selects meaningful time windows first and experiences second. It does not fill every calendar gap.

> Day context -> meaningful windows -> candidate experiences -> diversity and burden check -> quiet day rhythm

## Window rules

- Use broad human windows such as morning, midday, afternoon, and evening.
- Show an opportunity only when timing materially improves relevance.
- Include preparation, travel, activity, and return buffer in feasibility.
- Avoid stacking demands or implying that balance requires completing every card.
- Permit `nothing extra today` as a successful outcome.
- Do not infer that a calendar gap is free personal time without confirmation or learned permission.

## Presentation

Normally show two to four opportunities. One may be visually highlighted as the next meaningful opening; later opportunities remain compact. Each card includes a time window, Experience Promise, commitment, and one action.

Recipes, movement, restoration, connection, learning, culture, and nature appear because the time window and person make them relevant—not to cover categories.

## State

An opportunity may be proposed, held for later, accepted, started, declined for today, completed, or expired. Decline is situational. Expired live claims are never silently reused.

## Notification boundary

Holding an opportunity does not automatically authorize interruption. A reminder must be explicitly accepted, timely, and cancellable. Silence is the default.
