# Experience Loop

Status: Foundational  
Version: 1.0

Momentum is organized around a human loop, not a sequence of app screens:

> Understanding → Wonder → Momentum → Presence → Memory → renewed Understanding

## 1. Understanding — what fits now?

Momentum combines only the context it is permitted to use: time, place, availability, weather, energy, preferences, company, routines, explicit intent, and relevant changes in the world.

Output: a small set of plausible experiences, ranked with honest confidence.

## 2. Wonder — why would I want this?

The leading suggestion becomes an **Experience Promise**. It shows the likely feeling or memorable detail before explaining the algorithm.

Examples:

- “Over 45 minutes you could watch the light turn gold across the water.”
- “Five quiet minutes could help your body settle before sleep.”
- “This short kettlebell session should leave you feeling strong, not depleted.”
- “Your familiar morning shake fits the time you have and the energy you need.”

The promise must be attractive, specific, and truthful. Live claims need a reliable source and freshness indicator.

## 3. Momentum — make beginning effortless

After the user says yes, the suggestion expands into an **Experience Capsule**: a temporary, focused interface containing exactly what is needed to begin and continue.

Depending on the experience, that may include:

- what to take and a route handoff;
- ingredients, missing items, and one cooking step at a time;
- equipment, intervals, and one exercise at a time;
- a breathing rhythm, timer, and minimal guidance.

The transition should feel continuous: the promise grows into the preparation instead of opening an unrelated information page.

## 4. Presence — guide quietly

Once the experience is underway, Momentum reduces itself to the smallest useful presence. It may hand off to a trusted capability such as Maps or use a Live Activity, haptic cue, timer, or single next step.

Principle: leaving Momentum is not leaving the experience.

No feed, unrelated recommendation, upsell, or engagement loop may interrupt an active experience.

## 5. Memory — keep meaning, not administration

At an appropriate moment after the experience, Momentum may invite one lightweight reflection:

- Was this worth it?
- How do you feel now?
- Keep one photo?

The user may skip it. Completion can also be inferred cautiously from permitted signals, but Momentum must not claim an experience occurred without adequate evidence.

The memory updates understanding only with transparent, reversible signals such as preference, timing, felt outcome, and whether the suggestion was useful.

## Two entrances, one loop

### Passive intent

Momentum notices context and offers its best current suggestion.

### Active intent

The user supplies missing present-tense information, ideally through a small sheet rather than a library:

- **time:** 15 minutes, 30 minutes, one hour, tonight;
- **desired state:** calm, energy, wonder, connection, growth;
- **company:** alone, partner, family, friends;
- optional constraints.

Momentum then returns to Understanding and produces one adapted proposal. Active intent is not traditional search; it is a concise correction of context.

## Experience quality check

An experience is coherent only when:

1. the suggestion fits the available context;
2. the promise creates truthful desire;
3. execution is immediately understandable;
4. guidance decreases once action begins;
5. reflection is proportional to the experience;
6. learning preserves autonomy and privacy.
